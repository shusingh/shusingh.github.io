## Change intent

MarkItDown converts messy enterprise documents into Markdown for search, RAG, and agent workflows. Email was a meaningful gap. Teams often have important source material trapped in `.eml` files, and ignoring that format cuts retrieval systems off from a large part of the real corpus.

This PR adds an EML converter that extracts message headers, body content, and attachment metadata into Markdown.

## Requirements

- Parse RFC 822 / MIME email files.
- Preserve useful headers such as From, To, Subject, and Date.
- Prefer `text/plain` body content when available.
- Fall back to HTML content when plain text is absent.
- Surface attachment metadata without pretending attachments were fully converted.
- Add fixtures for single-part, multipart, and HTML-only messages.

The implementation treats the email as a structured document rather than a blob of text. Headers become a readable preamble. Body selection follows a deterministic preference order. Attachment metadata is included so downstream systems know what was present even if they do not ingest the attachment content yet.

## Design decisions

**Do not hide multipart complexity.** Email clients produce many shapes. The converter needs predictable behavior for single-part, multipart, and HTML-only messages.

**Preserve metadata for retrieval.** Sender, recipient, subject, and date can matter as much as the body text when an email becomes part of a knowledge corpus.

**Report attachments as metadata.** A converter should not silently discard attachments. Listing them gives downstream ingestion systems a hook for later processing.

## Status

Open as of submission. The PR includes converter fixtures and follows the existing library extension pattern so the integration surface stays small.
