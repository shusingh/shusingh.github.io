## What the PR adds

MarkItDown's whole job is turning messy enterprise documents into clean Markdown so downstream RAG systems and agents have something usable to work with. Emails were a gap: the library could handle PDFs, Office docs, screenshots, and HTML, but `.eml` files were unsupported.

This PR adds an EML converter that:

- Parses RFC 822 / MIME structure from the email envelope
- Extracts headers (From, To, Subject, Date) into a structured preamble
- Walks the multipart body, preferring `text/plain` and falling back to `text/html`
- Surfaces attachment metadata (filename, content type, size) in the converted output
- Ships with test fixtures covering single-part, multipart, and HTML-only messages

## Why it matters

Email is one of the most common "real production data" formats. If your retrieval system cannot ingest it, you cut yourself off from a large chunk of the corpus most teams already have. The fix is not conceptually hard, but the implementation has to handle every weird shape an old email client can produce.

## Status

Open as of submission. The PR includes new test coverage and follows the existing converter patterns in the codebase, so the integration surface should stay small.
