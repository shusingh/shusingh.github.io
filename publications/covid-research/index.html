An approach to forecast impact of Covid-19 using supervised machine learning model

Link to full Research Paper

The current focus on the analysis of environmental data for the prediction of future trends has made it a prime area of research worldwide. Based on the kind of data prediction and analytical techniques employed, the present as well as the future state of the data can be forecasted or predicted. The novelty of the work proposed and outlined in this article is that it considers an approach that combines nonlinear transmission and social-spatial and temporal transmission along with the monthwise prediction of future data. Most of the historical data-driven approaches have been linear methods, and do not consider the temporal or time-based transmission methods.

As such, the contribution of the article can be outlined thus:

  • Building of a computational hybrid model with long term predictions for India, with statewise and datewise views.
  • Proposing the ensemble learning hybrid model that integrates different machine learning techniques and improves prediction accuracy.
  • Applying an autoregressive correlation model to predict the future behavior of Covid-19 data using past and seasonal data.
  • Use of the hybrid model to predict the future Covid-19 status of various countries, along with statewise, datewise data views predicted with the help of seasonal data such as heat, air quality, location, and other dynamically updated inputs.
